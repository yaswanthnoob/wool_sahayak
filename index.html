Wool types

marino wool

Known for its exceptional softness and fine texture, Merino wool is sourced from Merino sheep. It's highly breathable, moisture-wicking, and offers excellent insulation, making it ideal for premium clothing and activewear.

Cashmere

Cashmere wool comes from the undercoat of Cashmere goats and is celebrated for its luxurious softness and lightweight warmth. It's often used to craft high-end sweaters, scarves, and shawls.

Lambswool

Lambswool is derived from the first shearing of young sheep, making it exceptionally soft and fine. It's commonly used in knitwear and blankets due to its comfort and warmth.

Alpaca Wool

Alpaca wool, obtained from the fleece of alpacas, is prized for its silky texture, warmth, and hypoallergenic properties. It's often used in luxurious sweaters, shawls, and accessories.

Steps involved in wool farm to market

Wool shearing

Wool shearing is the process of carefully removing the fleece from sheep, promoting their well-being and ensuring the sustainable production of versatile natural fibers. The collected wool is used to create various products, from clothing to insulation.

wool preperation

Wool preparation involves several steps such as washing, carding, and combing, which help refine the sheared wool and remove impurities, resulting in a clean, soft, and ready-to-spin fiber. This well-prepared wool is then used in the production of high-quality textiles and other wool-based products.

Wool processing

Wool processing encompasses several crucial stages, including washing, carding, spinning, and weaving. During this intricate process, raw sheared wool is transformed into refined yarns and fabrics, ready to be used in various textile applications. This meticulous processing ensures the wool's quality, texture, and durability in the final products.

Wool spinning

Wool spinning is the craft of twisting and stretching prepared wool fibers into yarn, a crucial step in textile production. This process ensures the wool's suitability for knitting, weaving, and crafting various woolen goods.

Fabric production

Wool fabric production involves weaving or knitting woolen yarns into textiles, followed by finishing processes like dyeing and brushing. This results in a versatile and warm fabric, highly valued for its durability, insulation properties, and comfort in a variety of clothing and textile applications.

Finishing and Distribution

In the finishing stage of wool processing, the fabric is treated to achieve desired characteristics like softness, sheen, and color. After finishing, the wool products are distributed through various channels, including wholesalers, retailers, and online platforms, ensuring availability for consumers in a wide range of markets and applications.
